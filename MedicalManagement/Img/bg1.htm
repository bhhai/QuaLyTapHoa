Independent pharmacist morale at all time low
By: Anonymous | Published On: 28/08/19 11:23 pm

Research by the National Pharmacy Association (NPA) has revealed that morale of independent pharmacists has dropped and pessimism is increasing.

The NPA's survey was completed by 238 contractors in England, ran from December 2018 to January 2019 and passed to the government in April.

The NPA analysis reported by Chemist and Druggist magazine showed that four in five (79%) independent pharmacist reported morale of their pharmacy team was lower than the previous year.

The NPA analysis found:

  1. Nine in ten independent pharmacists said they are less optimistic about the prospects for the sector than a year earlier.
  2. Respondents told the NPA that they “are working flat out, but are still unable to make ends meet.”
  3. 97% of respondents said they took home less than two years ago, while 64% say they put their own money into the business last year.

Conclusions drawn from the NPA survey suggested that with sinking morale and dwindling profits last year, the jury is still out on whether the new funding contract – and its promise of a shift to clinical services – will provide any respite for England’s independent pharmacies.
